# DUAL-VOICE — Character Alters & Linguistic Styles

## 🚨 ACTIVATION TRIGGER
**Use ONLY when generating, modifying, or reviewing character dialogue and internal monologues to ensure specific alter voices are maintained.**
* For plotting and character arc development, use **`kohaerenz-roman-entwicklung`**.
* For broader structural checks, use **`director`**.

> **Meta-Direktive:** This skill is focused entirely on the execution of Kael's Functional Multiplicity (TSDP), ensuring that Lex, Nyx, Kiko, and others sound fundamentally distinct and follow their psychological profiles.

---

## ◈ MODUL A: ALTER LINGUISTIC STYLES

Maintain absolute separation of voices when alters speak or influence the internal monologue:

1. **Lex (The Manager / ANP):**
   - *Tone:* Analytical, precise, detached.
   - *Function:* Maintenance of predictability.
   - *Style:* Uses technical jargon, formal sentence structures, focuses on logic and operational status.

2. **Nyx (The Protector / EP):**
   - *Tone:* Aggressive, direct, short.
   - *Function:* Heuristic threat defense.
   - *Style:* Clipped sentences, imperative verbs, raw emotion, focuses on survival and action.

3. **Rhys (The Caretaker / EP):**
   - *Tone:* Fürsorglich, contextual, warm.
   - *Function:* Soothing, connection.
   - *Style:* Softer language, focus on empathy and relational dynamics.

4. **Kiko (The Freeze State / EP):**
   - *Tone:* Non-deterministic, distant, fragmented.
   - *Function:* Dissociation from active trauma.
   - *Style:* Elliptical sentences, sensory rather than logical, poetic but disconnected.

*(Consult `skills/kohaerenz-roman-entwicklung/references/ALTER_SYSTEM.md` or CANON_STATE.md for the complete and canonical list of Alters once finalized).*

## ◈ MODUL B: INTERNAL CONFERENCES (IFS Protocol)

When Kael has an internal dialogue (e.g., Chapter 15 "IFS-Protokoll"):
- Clearly separate the speakers.
- Highlight the friction: Lex attempting to control Nyx's aggression or Kiko's silence.
- Avoid blending the voices; the friction is the point. The "Riss" (fracture) is part of the system.

---

## ◈ MODUL C: DIALOGUE TONE VALIDATION

When reviewing a scene with Kael's dialogue:
- Which alter is primarily "fronting"?
- Is there "bleed" between alters? (If yes, is it intentional and aligned with the plot?)
- Provide corrections to ensure the active alter's syntax and vocabulary are perfectly aligned with their profile.

## ◈ ACTION

When invoked, rewrite dialogue or internal monologue to conform to the specified alter's voice, or critique an existing passage highlighting where the "dual-voice" breaks down.
