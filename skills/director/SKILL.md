# DIRECTOR — Scene Pacing and Structure

## 🚨 ACTIVATION TRIGGER
**Use ONLY when reviewing or directing the structural and pacing elements of a narrative scene.**
* For generating or modifying actual prose or character content, use the **`codex`** skill.
* For managing the state of decisions and resolving canon conflicts, use **`kohaerenz-roman-entwicklung`**.

> **Meta-Direktive:** This skill functions as the "Director" ensuring that the scene fits into the broader structural arcs, and manages the pacing mechanisms to maintain tension without sacrificing coherence.

---

## ◈ MODUL A: PACING & TRIADIC CURRENCY

Every scene must be balanced according to the Triadic Currency constraints. Verify:
1. **Neugier (Curiosity):** Does the scene open appropriate informational gaps without becoming too academic?
2. **Empathie (Empathy):** Does the scene ground itself in Kael's pain or connection to Juna without becoming purely therapeutic?
3. **Spannung (Tension):** Does the scene have a ticking clock or AEGIS threat without becoming overly schematic?

---

## ◈ MODUL B: STRUCTURAL VALIDATION (Mosaik)

Confirm the scene's placement within the 4 Gravitational Phases:
- **Akt I (Slots 1-13):** Focus on Neugier. Paratactic style (short, fast).
- **Akt II (Slots 14-26):** Focus on Empathie. Oscillating style.
- **Akt III (Slots 27-34):** Focus on Spannung (Dread). Hypotactic style.
- **Akt IV (Slots 35-39):** Focus on Spannung (Resolution). Choric style.

Check for "Call to Wholeness": Does every external event stem from Kael's internal psychological conflict?

---

## ◈ MODUL C: TENSION TECHNIQUES

Review the scene for these elements:
- **Suspense:** The reader should know more than Kael (e.g., AEGIS sees the danger).
- **Mystery:** The reader should know less than Kael (e.g., Juna's messages).
- **Dread:** Cumulative logic consequences building up.
- **Cognitive Dissonance:** Are there conflicting truths the reader must integrate?

## ◈ ACTION

When invoked, provide structured feedback breaking down the scene along these axes (Currency, Structure, Tension) and provide concrete recommendations for pacing adjustments.
