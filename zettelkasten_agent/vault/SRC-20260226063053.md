### Step 2: Create Audit Framework (15 minutes)

For each file, you'll assess:

#### Clarity (1-10)
- Is the main point immediately clear?
- Can I understand it without re-reading?
- Is terminology well-defined?

#### Conciseness (1-10)
- Does it respect my time?
- Is every word necessary?
- Could I explain the same concept in fewer words?

#### Engagement (1-10)
- Is it interesting to read?
- Does it maintain my attention?
- Would I recommend this to someone else?

#### Structure (1-10)
- Is information front-loaded?
- Are sections logically organized?
- Can I scan quickly?

#### Usefulness (1-10)
- Can I apply this immediately?
- Are examples concrete?
- Does it answer my questions?

**Overall Score:** Average of 5 dimensions

### Step 5: Create Improvement Checklist (20 minutes)

Based on your audit and pattern analysis, create a prioritized checklist of improvements.

**Template:**

```markdown

**Created:** 2025-11-06
**Based on:** Writing Quality Audit
**Priority:** High to Low

---

### 1. [Most impactful improvement]
**Impact:** High
**Effort:** Low/Medium/High
**Applies to:** All content / Zettelkasten / Documentation / Skills / Quests

**Problem:**
[What's wrong currently]

**Solution:**
[What to do instead]

**Example:**
Before: [Bad example]
After: [Good example]

**Quick Check:**
- [ ] [How to verify this improvement]

---

### 2. [Second most impactful]
[Same structure as above]

---
```

**Prioritization criteria:**
1. Impact (How much does this improve quality?)
2. Effort (How easy is it to implement?)
3. Breadth (Does it help across all domains or just one?)

Aim for **high-impact, low-effort improvements at the top**.

## Tips for Success

### Be Honest
Don't sugarcoat. The goal is improvement, not validation. Find real issues.

### Be Specific
"This is confusing" is vague. "The main point isn't stated until paragraph 3" is actionable.

### Be Constructive
Always suggest a fix. Criticism without solution isn't helpful.

### Use Examples
Show, don't tell. Quote problematic passages and rewrite them.

### Trust Your Gut
If something feels off, it probably is. Document that feeling and figure out why.

## Need Help?

**Stuck on scoring?**
Compare to examples in other files. What makes one clearer than another?

**Not finding issues?**
Try reading aloud. Friction while reading = improvement opportunity.

**Overwhelmed by the audit?**
Do 3 files at a time. Take breaks between domains.

**Can't prioritize improvements?**
Ask: "If I could only fix ONE thing, what would have the biggest impact?"
