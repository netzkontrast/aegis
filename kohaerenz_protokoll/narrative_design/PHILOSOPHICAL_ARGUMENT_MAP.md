# Philosophical Argument Map: The Great Debate

**Quest:** [[philosophy.md]]
**Objective:** Outline the core philosophical conflict: Coherence (AEGIS) vs. Correspondence (Kael/Foundation).

## The Core Thesis
> **Kael's Truth:** *Real coherence comes not from the elimination of the paradox, but from its integration.*
> **AEGIS's Truth:** *Truth is consistency. Contradiction is error. Error must be purged.*

---

## Act 1: The Thesis (Safety in Consistency)
The world starts under the rule of AEGIS. Safety is paramount.
- **Scene 1-5:** Establish the "Safe World" (Kernwelt 1). Everything works. No pain.
    - *Argument:* "Why do you want to remember the pain? Look how clean it is here."
- **Scene 6-10:** The Cracks Appear (The Correspondence Theory intrudes).
    - *Counter-Argument:* "This safety is a lie. My pain is real."
- **Climax (Scene 13):** The Choice.
    - *Decision:* Kael chooses the painful truth over the safe lie.

## Act 2: The Antithesis (Chaos of Fragmentation)
The system breaks. Correspondence reigns, but it is chaotic (Trauma).
- **Argument:** "See? Truth destroys. Integration is impossible. You are broken."
- **Counter-Argument:** "Broken things can still function. We are multiple."

## Act 3: The Synthesis (Functional Multiplicity)

## Dialogue Audit: Key Phrases to Avoid
- **Avoid:** Academic philosophy lectures. No one says "Epistemology."
- **Use:** "Does it fit?" (Coherence) vs. "Is it real?" (Correspondence).
- **Use:** "Clean the data." (AEGIS) vs. "Feel the heat." (Kael).
