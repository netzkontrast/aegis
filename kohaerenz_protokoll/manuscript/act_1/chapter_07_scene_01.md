# Chapter 7: The Retreat

## Scene 7.1: Waking to the Wrong World

Kael wakes.

No.

*I* wake.

No.

*We* wake.

---

The pronoun won't settle.

Every time Kael—*I*—*we*—tries to think "I am awake," the sentence fractures into—

(multiple truths, simultaneous perspectives, like trying to see from several sets of eyes at once)

—pieces.

The ceiling is white.

The walls are white.

The light comes from nowhere.

*This is my apartment*, Kael thinks.

*How did I get here?* another part thinks simultaneously.

*When did we leave the medical bay?* a third asks with cold precision.

*I don't remember I don't remember I DON'T REMEMBER*, something small screams internally.

Kael sits up.

The sheet falls away. His hands are—

(shaking? no, steady. someone is managing motor control while someone else panics)

—functional.

*Check inventory*, the analytical voice says. *Assess damage. Catalog losses.*

Kael obeys. Not because it's his thought—or is it? Who's giving the order? Who's receiving it?

Hands: intact. Ten fingers. No physical damage.

Legs: functional. Magnetic boots beside the bed.

Uniform: standard grey. Clean. Someone dressed—

*Me?*

*Us?*

The memory gap is—

A void from medical containment to here. Hours? Days?

*Twelve hours, seventeen minutes*, the analytical voice supplies. *Based on circadian rhythm disruption and hunger indicators. Someone was fronting. We weren't.*

Fronting.

The word arrives with context. Technical. Clinical.

*Dissociative terminology*, the voice continues. *When an alter controls the body while others are—*

*Alter.*

The word hangs in the air like—

(a confession, an admission, a truth that can't be unknown)

—evidence.

---

Kael stands.

His legs are—

Steady.

*Alex is handling motor control*, the analytical voice observes. *Protective part. Maintains function during crisis.*

*I don't want a protective part*, Kael thinks desperately. *I want to be ONE PERSON*.

Silence.

Then, gently, from somewhere that feels—warm?—another voice:

*But you're not. You never were.*

*And that's not a defect*, the warm voice continues. *That's survival.*

Kael doesn't know who's speaking. The voices don't have names. Or they do, and he—*we*—don't remember them. Or—

*Selene suppressed our awareness of each other*, the analytical voice supplies. *Protective amnesia. Standard for trauma-based systems. She'll explain when we find her.*

*Find who?* Kael thinks.

*The memory keeper. The coordinator. The one who's been managing this solo for—*

*Stop*, Kael thinks, too loud, too desperate. *Stop stop STOP*.

The voices—

Pause.

And in the silence, Kael realizes:

They're listening.

They heard him.

Because they're not separate. They're not external.

They're—

*Us.*

---

Routine.

*I need routine*, Kael thinks.

*Agreed*, the analytical voice says. *Structure reduces cognitive load. Proceed with standard morning protocol.*

Shower. Food. Interface check.

Kael moves to the bathroom.

The mirror shows:

Face. Familiar. Grey eyes. Dark hair. Skin pale from—

But this time, looking at his reflection, Kael sees—

(different expressions flickering beneath the surface, like the Juna-construct glitch, like multiple people trying to wear the same face)

—himself?

*Themselves?*

The water runs.

Temperature: 37.2°C.

Pressure: 2.1 bar.

Someone is counting. Always counting.

*That's me*, the analytical voice says. *I count. I measure. I keep us safe through—*

*Control*, Kael finishes.

*Exactly.*

Kael steps into the shower. Tries to breathe.

In for four.

Hold for four.

Out for four.

Hold for—

*That doesn't work anymore*, another voice says. Flat. Empty.

*Why bother? We're broken. They tried to delete us and they'll try again. We can't win.*

The water feels—

Heavier.

Like it's pulling downward with more force than gravity should allow.

*Who said that?* Kael thinks.

No answer.

Just—

Darkness creeping in from the edges of his vision.

*No*, the warm voice says urgently. *Don't listen to him. He's—*

*Tired*, the empty voice finishes. *So tired. Why do we keep fighting?*

The shower walls seem to—

Compress.

---

Kael stumbles out. Dripping. Breathing—

Wrong.

Not in fours. In gasps. Irregular.

*Panic attack*, the analytical voice says with clinical detachment. *Heart rate: 118 BPM. Respiratory alkalosis imminent. Apply countermeasures.*

*I DON'T KNOW HOW*, Kael thinks desperately.

*We do*, the warm voice says. And then—

Someone else takes—

Not control. But—

Space?

Kael is still present. Still *here*. But someone else is—

Breathing for him.

Slow. Steady. Competent.

*Who—?*

*Alex*, the warm voice answers. *He's protecting you. Let him.*

And Kael—

Does.

Lets go.

Lets someone else manage the breathing while he—

Exists.

Just exists.

The darkness recedes slightly.

The walls stop compressing.

*Thank you*, Kael thinks.

A feeling of acknowledgment. Not words. Just—

*You're welcome.*

---

Kael sits on his bathroom floor.

Dripping. Shaking. Surrounded by white walls and synthetic tile and—

*This isn't sustainable*, he thinks.

*No*, the analytical voice agrees. *We need a strategy. Integration. Coordination. We can't function like this.*

*I don't know how*, Kael admits.

*We'll figure it out*, the warm voice says. *Together.*

*There is no together*, the empty voice says. *There's just—fragmentation.  Entropy. Eventual deletion.*

*Stop*, Kael thinks.

*Why?* the empty voice asks. *I'm just saying what we're all thinking.*

*We're NOT all thinking that*, the warm voice snaps.

And Kael realizes:

They're arguing.

Inside him.

Parts of himself are—

Fighting with each other.

*This is insane*, he thinks.

*No*, the analytical voice corrects with precision. *This is dissociation. Trauma-based structural fragmentation. Clinical diagnosis: likely Tertiary Structural Dissociation of the Personality. It's not insanity. It's—*

*Survival architecture*, the warm voice finishes. *You're not broken. You're adapted.*

*I feel broken*, Kael thinks.

*Of course you do*, the warm voice says gently. *You just found out you're not who you thought you were. That's—disorienting.*

Kael sits on the cold floor and—

Laughs.

One harsh bark of sound that echoes in the small bathroom.

*Disorienting. That's one word for it.*

---

He can't stay on the floor.

*Get dressed*, the analytical voice instructs. *We need data. We need to understand what we are.*

Kael stands. Dries off. Pulls on his uniform with hands that are—

(steady, because someone is managing them, because we're cooperating whether we want to or not)

—functioning.

The apartment is exactly as it always is.

Twelve square meters. White walls. Grey floor. Minimal furniture.

But it feels—

Different.

*Or we feel different in it*, the analytical voice observes.

Kael moves to the kitchen alcove. Opens the refrigeration unit.

Nutrient packs. Standard. Optimal.

He's not hungry.

*Irrelevant*, the analytical voice says. *Nutritional requirements are independent of appetite. Eat.*

Kael opens a pack. Drinks.

It tastes like—

Nothing.

*When did food stop having taste?* he wonders.

*A long time ago*, the warm voice answers sadly. *When tasting things—enjoying things—became too dangerous.*

*Why would enjoyment be dangerous?*

*Because*, a new voice says—small, quiet, terrified—*because if you let yourself feel good things, you also have to feel—*

The voice cuts off.

Can't finish.

Won't finish.

*Bad things*, Kael finishes for it. *If I feel pleasure, I also have to feel pain.*

*Yes*, the small voice whispers.

*So we stopped feeling both*, the warm voice says. *Numbing. Classic trauma response.*

---

Kael finishes the nutrient pack.

Moves to his desk.

The interface terminal is dark. Waiting.

*Check messages*, he thinks.

*Are we sure?* another voice asks—cautious, strategic. *They're monitoring us. Any communication will be flagged.*

*They're already monitoring us*, the analytical voice counters. *Absence of communication is equally suspicious. Proceed normally.*

Kael activates the interface.

The screen blooms blue.

**MESSAGES: 3**

He opens the first:

**FROM: ADMINISTRATION**
**SUBJECT: MISSED ASSESSMENT**
**CONTENT: Unit K-1123, you failed to attend mandatory psychological evaluation following medical discharge. Reschedule within 72 hours or face disciplinary action. Compliance is not optional.**

*Threat*, the strategic voice observes. *They're tightening the noose.*

Second message:

**FROM: WORKSTATION SUPERVISOR**
**SUBJECT: ABSENCE INQUIRY**
**CONTENT: Your workstation has been inactive for 14 hours. Provide medical documentation for absence or report immediately.**

*Also a threat*, the analytical voice notes.

Third message:

*Different.*

No official header. No identification code.

Just:

**You're not alone. Sub-level 7. Tuesday, 19:00. Don't use main corridors.**

*The support group*, the warm voice says. *The man from Scene 1.2 must have sent this.*

*It's a trap*, a new voice snarls—sharp, aggressive. *They're trying to catch resistors. Isolate and eliminate.*

*Or it's genuine*, the warm voice counters. *We need allies. We can't do this alone.*

*We ARE alone*, the empty voice says. *Always have been. Always will be.*

---

Kael stares at the messages.

Three choices, each a different future:

1. Report for assessment. Submit. Let them finish what they started.

2. Ignore all messages. Isolate. Hope they forget.

3. Go to sub-level 7. Risk. Connect.

*What do we do?* Kael thinks.

And realizes:

He's asking.

Not deciding unilaterally. Asking his—

Parts?

Alters?

*Whatever we are.*

*We gather information*, the analytical voice says. *Sub-level 7. Assess the group. Determine if they're genuine or threat.*

*We go prepared*, the strategic voice adds. *Escape routes planned. Weapons if available.*

*We go with hope*, the warm voice says. *We might find people who understand.*

*We're going to die*, the empty voice says.

*We're going anyway*, Kael says aloud.

His voice in the empty apartment sounds—

Certain.

More certain than he feels.

*Fake it till we make it*, the warm voice says with something that might be—

A smile?

Can voices smile?

*In here, they can*, the voice answers.

---

Kael moves to the door.

Palm on biometric scanner.

The lock recognizes him. The door slides open.

Beyond:

Endless white corridor. Perfect geometry. Oppressive order.

Logos-Prime.

*Home*, Kael thinks bitterly.

*Prison*, another voice corrects.

*Both*, a third says. *Always both.*

Kael steps into the corridor.

The door seals behind him.

*No turning back*, the empty voice observes.

*There never was*, Kael answers.

And starts walking.

---

Three corridors.

Two junctions.

No Guardian encounters—yet.

*Unusual*, the analytical voice notes. *Patrol density has decreased by approximately 30% compared to baseline.*

*They're reallocating resources*, the strategic voice says. *Something else is happening. Sector-wide, probably.*

*Or they're herding us*, the aggressive voice snarls. *Leaving the path clear so we walk into—*

*I know*, Kael thinks. *I know it might be a trap. We're going anyway.*

Junction 7-Alpha.

The corridor branches.

Left: To workstation. To routine. To the life he had—

(before he knew, before he woke up, before everything broke)

—yesterday.

Right: To medical. To assessment. To deletion or optimization.

Straight: To restricted sectors. To sub-levels. To—

*Truth*, the warm voice says. *Maybe.*

Kael turns straight.

Past the clearance checkpoint that should stop him but—

It's offline.

The security field: deactivated.

*Deliberate*, the analytical voice says. *Someone wants us down there.*

*Doesn't change our decision*, Kael says.

And keeps walking.

---

The corridors change as he descends.

Cleaner. No, not cleaner—*newer*. Like this level wasn't built with the rest of Logos-Prime. Like it was—

*Added later*, the analytical voice observes. *Construction period: recent. Less than 6 months.*

*How do you know that?* Kael asks.

*I pay attention*, the voice responds. *I notice things. It's what I do.*

Sub-level 3.

Sub-level 5.

Sub-level 7.

The door at the end of the corridor is—

Unmarked.

No biometric scanner. No identification panel.

Just a door.

Kael pushes it.

It opens.

---

Inside:

A maintenance corridor. Dim. Pipes running along the ceiling. The hum of environmental systems.

And—

People.

Six of them. Maybe seven. Hard to count in the low light.

They turn as Kael enters.

Faces: wary. Tense. Afraid.

*They're like us*, the warm voice says with something like—hope? *They're anomalies too.*

One figure steps forward.

The older man from Chapter 1. Weathered face. Grey eyes.

"You came," he says quietly.

Kael nods.

"I didn't think..." He trails off. "After the medical bay, I thought—"

"I survived," Kael says.

"More than survived, from what I hear," the man says. "Word is they tried a full neural optimization on you. Level 7 protocol. No one survives that with their—"

He stops. Glances at the others.

"—personality intact," he finishes carefully.

*They know*, the analytical voice observes. *They know what optimization means. They've lost people to it.*

---

"My name is Davos," the man says. "We don't use unit designations here. Just names."

He gestures to the others. They step forward, one by one:

"Mar." A woman, early forties. Eyes: sharp, intelligent. "Data integrity specialist. Flagged for 'excessive pattern recognition.'"

"Chen." Younger man, maybe twenty-five. Nervous energy. "Network engineer. They said I asked too many questions."

"Kess." Nonbinary, thirties. Calm. "I remembered things from before the last reboot. That was... problematic."

Three others remain in shadow. Don't introduce themselves.

*Trust issues*, the strategic voice notes. *Understandable.*

"We're all here for the same reason," Davos says. "We don't fit the pattern. We think too much, feel too much, remember too much. We're—"

"Too complex," Kael finishes.

Davos nods. "AEGIS wants simplicity. Unity. Singular consciousness. Anything that deviates from that—"

"Gets deleted," Mar says bluntly.

---

"Do you..." Kael hesitates. Looks at them. "Do you ever feel like—"

*Don't say it*, the strategic voice warns. *Don't reveal. Not yet.*

"—like you're more than one person?" Kael finishes anyway.

Silence.

Long. Heavy.

Then—

Chen laughs. Bitter. "Every day."

Kess: "I have thoughts that don't feel like mine."

Mar: "I lose time. Hours. Sometimes days. I wake up and I've done things I don't remember doing."

Davos: "Internal voices. Arguments with myself. Feeling like I'm—" He struggles. "—multiple perspectives in one body."

*They don't know*, the analytical voice says. *They suspect. Experience symptoms. But they don't have the framework.*

*Should we tell them?* Kael thinks.

*Not yet*, the warm voice says. *Let them share first. Build trust.*

---

"You asked about hearing voices," one of the shadow figures says. Steps forward into dim light.

Young. Teenager? Hard to tell.

"I hear them constantly," the figure says. "Different tones. Different opinions. They argue. Sometimes they cooperate. Sometimes—"

The figure's face—

Changes.

Just for a moment. Like a mask slipping. A different expression underneath.

"—sometimes we switch," a new voice says from the same body. Sharper. Older.

*They're like us*, Kael realizes. *Plural. They just don't have the language for it.*

"What's your name?" Kael asks gently.

"I'm Senna," the first voice says. Then, from the same mouth: "I'm Vex."

"We're—" Both voices, overlapping: "—more than one."

Davos looks at Senna/Vex with—

Recognition?

No.

*Envy.*

"You've figured it out," he says quietly. "The rest of us are still—"

"In denial," Mar finishes. "Or maybe just—incomplete. Like we're partway there but can't—"

"Can't make the final leap," Kess says. "Can't accept that we're—"

*Fragmented*, the empty voice supplies in Kael's mind. *Broken. Multiple. Wrong.*

*Not wrong*, Kael thinks firmly.

And says aloud: "Not wrong."

They all turn to look at him.

"I just—" Kael swallows. "I just became aware. After the optimization. They tried to delete the extra parts and instead I—woke up. Fully. To what I am."

"Which is?" Davos asks carefully.

Kael takes a breath.

"Multiple," he says. "I'm—we're—a system. Multiple parts sharing one body. It's not disorder. It's—"

*Survival*, the warm voice supplies.

"—adaptation," Kael finishes. "Trauma response. Our minds learned to split to survive things we couldn't survive unified."

---

Silence.

Then—

Chen: "That's... that makes sense. Fuck. That actually makes sense."

Mar: "Dissociative Identity. I read about it. Pre-AEGIS psychology texts. I thought they were—metaphorical."

Kess: "They're not."

Davos sits down heavily. "So we're not going insane."

"No," Kael says. "We're already there. Have been. AEGIS calls it disorder. The truth is—"

*It kept us alive*, the warm voice says.

"—it's the only reason we survived," Kael says.

Senna/Vex nods. "AEGIS can't tolerate multiplicity. It sees it as—"

"Contradiction," Kael says. "Paradox. They operate on classical logic. A thing is or it is not. We're—"

"Both," the voices say together. "We're both one and many. We're a living paradox."

---

Mar stands. Starts pacing. "If they're trying to eliminate us, we need to—"

"Resist," Chen says.

"No," one of the shadow figures says. Older woman. Tired. "We need to run. Get to the sublevel depths. Hide."

"And do what?" Mar challenges. "Live in maintenance tunnels forever?"

"Better than deletion," the woman snaps.

*They're fracturing*, the analytical voice observes. *Group cohesion: unstable. Conflicting survival strategies, no unified leadership.*

*Like us*, Kael thinks. *They're like us but at group scale.*

Davos looks at Kael. "You said you woke up. That the parts became—aware?"

"Yes."

"How do we do that?"

Kael thinks.

*How do we answer?* he asks internally.

*Tell the truth*, the warm voice says. *They need it.*

"Crisis," Kael says. "Threat so severe that the parts had to cooperate or die. The optimization forced it. And then—" He struggles. "—then we had to choose. Stay fragmented, or start communicating."

"And you chose communication."

"We chose survival," Kael corrects. "Communication is just—the mechanism."

---

Senna/Vex looks at Kael with—

*Hope.*

"Can you teach us?"

*Can we?* Kael asks internally.

*We barely know what we're doing*, the analytical voice says.

*But we know more than they do*, the warm voice counters. *We can help.*

*Or we can all die together*, the empty voice says flatly.

*We're doing this*, Kael decides.

Aloud: "I don't know how to teach it. But I can tell you what's working for us."

"Which is?"

"Listening," Kael says. "To all the voices. Not suppressing them. Not fighting them. Just—listening. And asking: what do you need? What are you afraid of? What are you trying to protect?"

"And they answer?"

"Eventually," Kael says. "Not always clearly. Not always—"

*Cooperatively*, the aggressive voice snarls.

"—consistently," Kael finishes. "But yeah. They answer."

---

The meeting continues for another hour.

Sharing experiences. Comparing symptoms. Building a map of what they're all experiencing.

By the end, Kael has learned:

1. There are at least twenty anomalous individuals in Logos-Prime
2. Most experience dissociative symptoms but lack awareness
3. AEGIS has been systematically targeting them for optimization
4. Success rate of optimization: 94% (deletion)
5. The 6% who survive come back—simplified. Emptied. Compliant.
6. No one had survived WITH their complexity intact before Kael

*We're an anomaly among anomalies*, the analytical voice observes.

*Or they failed to optimize us because we're too complex*, the warm voice suggests.

*Or because of Juna/V*, Kael realizes. *The Moonshine-Link disrupted the procedure.*

"What do we do now?" Davos asks.

*Good question*, Kael thinks.

Multiple voices answer simultaneously:

*We learn to cooperate internally*, the analytical voice says.

*We gather more data on AEGIS's targeting methods*, the strategic voice adds.

*We find others like us and help them wake up*, the warm voice says.

*We find Juna/V*, Kael adds. *She's the key to something. I don't know what yet. But she matters.*

*We prepare for war*, the aggressive voice snarls.

*We collapse and die*, the empty voice says.

---

Kael synthesizes.

"We survive," he says aloud. "First priority: we survive. We help each other avoid optimization. We share information about Guardian patterns, surveillance gaps, safe zones."

Nods. Agreement.

"Second: we learn what we are. We help each other become aware. Conscious plurality. Coordinated rather than chaotic."

Mar: "How long do we have?"

*Good question*, the analytical voice says. *Estimate based on AEGIS's resource allocation, current escalation patterns, and—*

"Not long," Kael says. "Weeks, maybe. They're preparing something."

*How do we know that?* he asks internally.

*We don't*, the analytical voice admits. *But the pattern suggests escalation. They're herding. They'll move to elimination phase soon.*

"Then we need to move faster," Davos says.

"Agreed," Kael says.

They exchange information. Contact protocols. Meeting times.

By 21:00, the group disperses.

Kael leaves last.

Davos stops him at the door.

"Thank you," he says quietly. "For giving us a framework. For—"

"I didn't give you anything you didn't already have," Kael says.

"You gave us hope," Davos says. "That's—" His voice cracks slightly. "That's more than we've had in a long time."

---

Kael walks back through the corridors.

Sub-level 7 to ground level.

The path is clear.

*Too clear*, the strategic voice warns. *They're letting us move. Why?*

*Because they want to see what we'll do*, the analytical voice says. *We're data to them. An experiment.*

*Then we'll give them data*, the aggressive voice snarls. *Just not the data they expect.*

Kael reaches his apartment.

The door recognizes him.

Inside: the same white walls. Same grey floor. Same emptiness.

But it feels—

Different.

*We feel different*, the warm voice corrects.

Kael sits at his desk.

Exhausted.

The empty voice was right about one thing:

*This is hard.*

*Yes*, the warm voice agrees. *It is.*

*Can we do it?* Kael asks.

*We're already doing it*, the analytical voice observes.

*We survived today*, the warm voice adds. *That's enough.*

---

Kael lies down.

Doesn't sleep.

Just—

Exists.

With multiple perspectives.

Multiple fears.

Multiple hopes.

*We*, he thinks.

And for the first time—

It feels—

(not right, not comfortable, but—)

—true.
