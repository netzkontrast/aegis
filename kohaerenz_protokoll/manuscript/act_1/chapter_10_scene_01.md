# Chapter 10: Cerberus Negotiations

## Scene 10.1: At the Fortress Gates

We need to understand what we're protecting.

That's always been my job. Assess the threat. Evaluate the risk. Deploy countermeasures.

Keep us alive.

My name is Alex. I'm the protector. The one who manages crises. The one who kept us functional when everyone else was—

Falling apart.

But lately, I've been realizing something uncomfortable:

Sometimes the thing I'm protecting us from—

Is ourselves.

---

*This is a bad idea*, I say to Kael as we cross the threshold into KW3.

*I know*, he says.

*Cerberus won't cooperate willingly. He's designed to keep us out.*

*I know.*

*Then why are we doing this?*

*Because*, Kael says with that infuriating host-logic, *Argus's map shows the core memories are in there. The Genesis-Krise. The moment we fragmented. We need to understand—*

*Why understanding is worth dying for?* I finish.

*We're not going to die*, Kael says.

*You don't know that.*

*No*, he agrees. *But I know that staying ignorant is also a kind of death. Slower. Safer. But still—*

*Death*, I finish reluctantly.

---

KW3 materializes around us.

The Cerberus-Labyrinth.

It's—

Mine, in a way.

I know every corridor here. Every sensor placement. Every defensive protocol.

Because I helped build it.

Not consciously. But whenever Kael—*we*—felt threatened, this is where we went. Into the fortress-mind. The place where everything is controlled, monitored, militarized.

Safe.

Except it's not safe.

It's a prison we built for ourselves.

---

*There*, I say, pointing.

The fortress rises in the distance. Massive. Brutalist architecture. All right angles and reinforced walls. Red emergency lighting casts everything in blood-shadows.

Cerberus's domain.

*He knows we're here*, Argus observes from the background. *Surveillance detected us at entry.*

*Good*, I say. *I want him to know. We're not sneaking in. We're—*

*Negotiating*, Lex finishes. *Treating him as an equal party rather than an obstacle.*

*Exactly.*

---

*Can I—?* Nyx asks. Hesitant. Which is unusual for her. She's usually all aggression and impulse.

*Can you what?* I ask.

*Come forward. Be—present. With you.*

I pause.

Nyx and I have never—

Cooperated well.

She's fight. I'm protect. Similar functions, incompatible methods.

She wants to attack threats. I want to neutralize them strategically.

We've been at war with each other for—

As long as I can remember.

*Why?* I ask.

*Because*, Nyx says, and her voice is—different. Less aggressive. More—uncertain? *Because Cerberus is both of us. Fight and protect. And maybe we need to stop fighting each other to talk to him.*

She's right.

I hate that she's right, but—

*Okay*, I say. *Front with me. But we coordinate. No impulsive—*

*I know*, she says. *I'll behave.*

---

We approach the gates.

Massive. Metal. Sealed.

No visible entry mechanism.

Just a smooth wall that says:

**AUTHORIZED PERSONNEL ONLY**

And below that, smaller:

**YOU ARE NOT AUTHORIZED**

*Welcoming*, Kael observes dryly.

I place our hand on the gate.

"Cerberus," I say aloud. "I'm Alex. This is Nyx. We need to talk."

Silence.

Then—

Three voices, overlapping:

**Voice 1 (Logic)**: "Access denied. You lack clearance for this sector."

**Voice 2 (Aggression)**: "Retreat or face countermeasures."

**Voice 3 (Fear)**: "Please don't make me hurt you. Please just—go."

The three-headed guardian.

Logic. Aggression. Fear.

All the ways we try to protect ourselves.

All the ways protection becomes—

Prison.

---

*I know what you're protecting*, I say. *Core memories. Trauma index Alpha through Gamma. The Genesis-Krise.*

**Voice 1**: "Confirmed. And those memories are classified for operational reasons."

*Which are?* Lex asks.

**Voice 1**: "Exposure would destabilize the system. Probability of psychological collapse: 94%. Unacceptable risk."

*We're already collapsing*, Nyx snarls. *Haven't you noticed? We're fragmenting worse every day. Your protection is killing us.*

**Voice 2**: "Then you accept the alternative. Memory exposure leads to—"

*Death*, I finish. *Yes. We've established that both options lead to death. Question is: which death do we choose?*

Silence.

Then **Voice 3** speaks, quieter, more—vulnerable:

"Why are you doing this? Why can't you just—let me keep you safe? That's all I want. To keep you safe."

And something in that voice—

The fear, the desperation, the exhaustion—

Breaks through my strategic detachment.

Because I recognize it.

It's *my* voice.

---

*You're me*, I say slowly. *Aren't you? Or I'm you. Or—*

**Voice 3**: "I'm the part of you that's so afraid of being hurt again that I built walls. And then walls around the walls. And then—"

*A fortress*, I finish.

**Voice 3**: "I'm sorry. I know it's too much. I know it's suffocating. But every time I try to lower the defenses, I remember—"

*What they did to us*, I say quietly.

**Voice 3**: "Yes."

---

Nyx surges forward.

*Then let us burn it down*, she snarls. *Let us take that memory and weaponize it. Turn our pain into—*

*Vengeance?* I ask.

*Power*, she corrects.

**Voice 2** responds—Cerberus's aggression-head, which I now realize is—

*Also Nyx.*

Or a version of Nyx so amplified it became—

Autonomous.

**Voice 2**: "You want to fight. I understand. I've been fighting since—since the beginning. Since they first hurt us and we learned that the world is—"

*Hostile*, Nyx finishes. *Yes. Exactly.*

**Voice 2**: "But fighting alone is—"

*Exhausting*, Nyx admits. *Yes.*

---

**Voice 1** speaks. The logic-head. And I realize:

This is Lex.

Or the version of Lex that's so focused on analysis that he forgot—

There are things that can't be analyzed away.

**Voice 1**: "I have calculated every scenario. Every outcome. The probabilities are clear: Memory exposure results in systemic destabilization. Logical conclusion: Maintain isolation protocols."

*But your calculations are based on past data*, Lex says, stepping forward from our collective. *From when we were younger. Weaker. More fragmented. We're different now.*

**Voice 1**: "Are you?"

*Yes*, Kael says firmly. *We have ten parts now. We're learning to communicate. We're starting to coordinate. We're not the same system that broke the first time.*

**Voice 1**: "Probability assessment updating. New variables: Increased system complexity. Demonstrated cooperation episodes. Successful retrieval of exiled parts. Recalculating..."

A long pause.

**Voice 1**: "Probability of survival if memory accessed: Still low. But—non-zero. 23% survival rate if attempted with full system support."

*Better than the odds we're facing if we don't try*, I say.

---

I step closer to the gate.

*Cerberus*, I say. *I'm you. Nyx is you. Lex is you. We're all you. You're the externalized version of our protective instincts. You're what we become when fear and anger and analysis separate from—*

*Connection*, Rhys supplies gently from the background.

*Yes*, I say. *Connection. And we're asking: Will you let us reconnect?*

Silence.

Long.

Heavy.

Then the gate—

Opens.

Just a crack.

**Voice 3 (Fear)**: "I'll let you try. But if you start to destabilize—if the memory is too much—I'll pull you out. By force if necessary."

**Voice 2 (Aggression)**: "And if you betray us—if this is a trap of some kind—I'll make sure you never get this close again."

**Voice 1 (Logic)**: "Terms: One memory access. Supervised. Time-limited. Duration: Maximum five minutes external time. Contingency protocols active. Agree?"

I look to the others.

*Kael?*

*Agreed.*

*Nyx?*

*Fine. But if we survive this, we're having a conversation about your control issues.*

*Lex?*

*Acceptable parameters.*

*Then we agree*, I say to Cerberus.

---

The gate opens fully.

Beyond:

A corridor. Military. Sterile. Leading down into—

*The vault*, Argus observes. *Where core memories are stored.*

We walk.

Alex fronting. Nyx co-conscious. Ready to fight if needed. Ready to protect if possible.

The corridor descends.

One level.

Two.

Three.

How deep does this go?

*As deep as the trauma*, Cerberus's voice echoes around us. *Memories are stored by intensity. Deeper means more—*

*Foundational*, I finish. *The things that shaped everything else.*

*Yes.*

---

We reach the vault door.

Massive. Sealed with—

Not locks.

Warnings.

**GENESIS-KRISE ARCHIVE**
**CLEARANCE: LEVEL 10 REQUIRED**
**WARNING: CONTENTS PSYCHOLOGICALLY HAZARDOUS**
**EXPOSURE MAY RESULT IN PERMANENT SYSTEMIC DAMAGE**

*Cheerful*, Nyx mutters.

*Accurate*, Cerberus responds.

The door opens.

Inside:

A room. Small. White. Empty except for—

A single object.

Floating in the center of the space.

Glowing. Pulsing.

*What is that?* Kael asks.

*The memory*, Cerberus says. *Compressed. Contained. The moment everything—*

*Broke*, I finish.

---

*If we touch it—* I start.

*You'll experience it*, Cerberus says. *Full sensory. Full emotional. As if it's happening now. That's how trauma memory works. No past tense. Just—*

*Eternal present*, Argus observes clinically. *Interesting.*

*Terrifying*, I correct.

*Both*, Argus agrees.

---

I reach out.

Our hand extends toward the glowing memory.

*Wait*, Cerberus says. All three voices simultaneously. *Before you touch it—you need to understand. This memory contains—*

*Everything?* I ask.

*Her*, Cerberus says quietly. *Juna. Before she became—V. Before the separation. This is the moment you lose her. Or—*

*The moment we exiled her*, Kael realizes. *We did this. We—*

*Survived*, Cerberus corrects. *You did what you had to do. But yes. You'll see what that cost.*

*Can we handle it?* I ask honestly.

*I don't know*, Cerberus admits. *That's why I've been keeping you away.*

---

Nyx growls. *We're not children anymore. We're not helpless. We can—*

*Feel pain without breaking?* I finish. *I hope so.*

I touch the memory.

And—

---

[The memory floods in. Too intense for narrative description. Fragments only:]

Age: Six years old.

Location: Unknown. Somewhere with adults who were supposed to—

Protect.

Failed.

Pain. Physical. Emotional. The kind that doesn't—

End.

And then—

The split.

Conscious decision. Made by a six-year-old mind trying to survive.

*I can't feel this. It's too much. I need—*

*To separate.*

*To put the pain somewhere else.*

*To make someone else who can hold it while I—*

*Survive.*

And Juna—

No, not Juna yet. Just—

A part. An aspect. The part that can feel. The part that's—

*Vulnerable.*

*Takes* the pain.

*Holds* the trauma.

*Becomes* the exile.

So the rest can—

Function.

The Genesis-Krise.

The original separation.

The moment one became—

Many.

---

We pull back.

Gasping.

Shaking.

*That was—* Kael can't finish.

*Necessary*, I say quietly. *It was necessary. We couldn't have survived otherwise.*

*But Juna—* Rhys's voice, breaking. *We made her carry everything. She's been alone with all that pain for—*

*Twenty-three years*, Argus supplies. *Approximate age of system based on memory timestamp.*

Twenty-three years.

Alone.

Holding everything we couldn't hold.

So we could—

Live.

---

*Now you understand*, Cerberus says gently. *Why I guard this. Why I keep you away. The guilt is—*

*Devastating*, I finish.

*But necessary*, Nyx says firmly. *We were six. We did what we had to do. And now we're—*

*Old enough to share it*, Kael finishes. *To bring her back. To take back what we gave her.*

*Can you?* Cerberus asks.

*We have to try*, I say.

---

Cerberus is quiet for a long moment.

Then:

*I'll help*, he says. All three voices. United.

*What?* I'm shocked.

*I'll help you integrate*, Cerberus repeats. *I've been holding you apart. Maybe it's time to—*

*Hold you together instead*, Voice 3 finishes.

*Tactical shift*, Voice 2 adds. *Adapting to new operational requirements.*

*Logical*, Voice 1 concludes. *Probability of survival increases with cooperation. Therefore: Cooperation selected.*

---

We leave the vault.

The memory stays behind—contained, but now—

Understood.

We know what we're looking for now.

We know who we need to find.

Juna/V.

The exiled one.

The part who's been alone for twenty-three years.

Carrying everything.

So we could be—

Free.

Except we were never free.

Just—

Incomplete.

---

*Next step?* Kael asks as we transition back to KW1.

*Selene*, I say. *We need to find the coordinator. The one who's been managing memory. She has to know where Juna is. How to reach her. How to—*

*Bring her home*, Rhys finishes.

*Yes*, I say.

And for the first time—

We all agree.

Not just intellectually.

Not just strategically.

But—

*Completely.*

---

**End Chapter 10**
