## Key Narrative Techniques Used

### 1. Ambiguous Internal Voices
```markdown
Before: *That's normal*, Lex supplies, calm and analytical.
After: *That's normal*, he thinks, forcing certainty into the words.
```

### 2. Automatic Processes Without Understanding
```markdown
Before: *I can handle the fear*, Alex says. *Route it to me. I'll process it.*
After: The suffocating terror *shifts*. Like a weight being lifted and—
        (carried, where? by what? by who?)—distributed.
        *How do I do that?* he wonders.
```

### 3. Presences Felt But Not Recognized
```markdown
Kael feels something strange. A presence in the background of his awareness.
Monitoring his biosigns, keeping his heart rate steady.
Another presence: running scenarios...
*This isn't real*, Kael tells himself. *This is psychological fragmentation.*
```

### 4. "We" as Confused Self-Reference
```markdown
Before: *We're really doing this*, Kiko whispers.
After: And Kael—(we?)—doesn't know if hiding is even possible anymore.
```

### 5. Rationalization of Symptoms
```markdown
*Post-reboot errors*, he tells himself firmly.
*Memory consolidation issues. Psychological stress from the hallucination.
I'm experiencing temporary cognitive fragmentation.*
```

## Reader Experience Design

### Dramatic Irony
The revision creates powerful dramatic irony where:
- **Reader knows** (or strongly suspects) Kael is plural
- **Kael doesn't know** - interprets symptoms as malfunction
- Creates tension: "When will he discover the truth?"

### Clues for Attentive Readers
Readers familiar with plurality may recognize:
- Different "qualities" of thought (analytical, warm, angry, small)
- Automatic emotional regulation
- Memory gaps and "lost time"
- Internal arguments with distinct perspectives
- Somatic responses (body knowing things mind doesn't)
- Dreams of "parts" in containment
- Guardian's specific questions about "voices"

### Mystery Maintained
For readers unfamiliar with plurality:
- Is Kael malfunctioning or discovering something?
- Are the "other worlds" real or hallucinations?
- Is he broken or complex?
- What did AEGIS do to him?
