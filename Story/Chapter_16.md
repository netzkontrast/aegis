## Scene 16.2: The Discursive Ruse (Leitfrage 5)

**Location**: Sector 9 Data Core Access
**POV**: Polyphonic (Lex / Nyx / Alex)
**Active Alters**: Lex (Main Console), Nyx (Distraction), Alex (Coordinator)

**Synopsis**:
The Council needs to bypass a Level 5 Guardian to access the deep archives.
*   **The Plan**: AEGIS's D2 Logic Module (Discursive Logic) treats each Alter as a separate, uncoordinated "speaker." It looks for *coherence* (patterned action) to identify a threat.
*   **The Execution**:
    *   *Lex (KW1)*: Starts a complex, nonsensical mathematical query at the main terminal. Pure abstract theory.
    *   *Nyx (KW3)*: Simultaneously triggers a "malfunction" in a nearby maintenance hatch. Banging, shouting, "random" rage.
    *   *Alex (Internal)*: "Hold pattern. 3... 2... 1."
*   **AEGIS's Blind Spot**: The system sees two unrelated events: "User Query: Math" and "Mechanical Failure: Impact." It dispatches a low-level maintenance drone for Nyx and a chatbot for Lex.
*   **The Exploit**: In the 0.4 seconds of processing lag between the two disparate events, Alex slips the "Juna-Key" (the irreducible data) into the core.
*   **Result**: Access granted. The system didn't see the *third hand*.
