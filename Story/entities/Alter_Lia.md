# Lia: The Connector

## Core Identity
**Role**: **EP (Emotional Part)**
**TSDP Action System**: Attachment (Ambivalent)
**Core Motivation**: Desire for safe, playful connection.
**Core Fear**: Being hurt through connection, but also isolation.

## Function
Lia embodies the system's need for relationship and connection, often manifesting as an ambivalent push-pull dynamic.

## Narrative Progression

### Act 1: The Longing
*   **Chapter 1-4**: Her presence is felt as a subtle "tug" or desire for connection amidst the sterile order of **Logos-Prime**.
*   **Chapter 12**: Attends **The First Gathering** but may struggle with trust.

### Act 2: The Complication
*   **Chapter 19**: Her "ambivalent attachment" complicates the connection to **Juna/V**.
*   **Chapter 21**: Her fear of "being hurt through connection" creates obstacles in the **Mnemosyne-Archipel**.

### Act 3: The Bond
*   **Chapter 33**: Finds a secure attachment within the integrated system, no longer fearing abandonment.
*   **Chapter 39**: Becomes a key part of the new **Functional Multiplicity**, fostering internal harmony.
